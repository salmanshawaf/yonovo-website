Chaser and Yonovo are both AR automation platforms, both connect to your accounting system, and both automate the work of chasing unpaid invoices. On paper they cover similar ground. In practice they were built in different markets for different teams.

Chaser is a UK-based platform with strong roots in the Xero ecosystem, an emphasis on cash flow forecasting, and an optional outsourced AR specialist service for teams that want a human in the loop. Yonovo is a US-based platform with a free tier, published flat pricing, and a setup that connects to your accounting system in a day.

This guide breaks down where the two differ on pricing, setup, channels, integrations, forecasting, and fit, so you can decide which one matches your team.

## Positioning and target customer

The clearest difference between Chaser and Yonovo is the market each one is built for.

Chaser is a UK company that prices in GBP, leads with cash flow forecasting, and runs an active partner program for accountants and bookkeepers. Its customer base is SMB-heavy, with a particular concentration in the Xero ecosystem. Reviewers on Capterra and G2 consistently highlight the Xero integration and the "personal touch" approach to chasing customers. Chaser claims more than 10,000 users worldwide.

Yonovo was built for US-based businesses, prices in USD, and integrates broadly across [QuickBooks](/solutions/quickbooks), [Xero](/solutions/xero), [NetSuite](/solutions/netsuite), [Sage Intacct](/solutions/sage), [Odoo](/solutions/odoo), FreshBooks, Salesforce, HubSpot, and SAP. Our customers include [manufacturers](/industries/manufacturing), [wholesale distributors](/industries/wholesale-distribution), [professional services firms](/industries/professional-services), [construction companies](/industries/construction), and SaaS businesses. The common thread is smaller finance teams chasing payments across many invoices, with limited bandwidth for a long implementation.

**If you are a UK or European SMB, run on Xero, and want a vendor with a strong accountant partner channel, Chaser is a reasonable fit.** If you are a US-based business that wants a free tier, published pricing, and collections automated today, Yonovo is built for that.

## Pricing and total cost

Chaser publishes four tiers, all priced in GBP and gated by company revenue:

- **Compact** from £199/month: 4 users, 30 follow-up templates, 4 automated workflows, revenue under £4M
- **Core** from £599/month: unlimited users, templates, and workflows, multi-entity support, revenue under £10-20M depending on currency
- **Complete** from £899/month: Core plus dedicated account manager, priority support, assisted onboarding, receivables forecast, revenue under £100M
- **Custom**: quote-based, for revenue above the Complete cap
- **Care** add-on from £324/month: dedicated AR specialist for outsourced follow-ups, phone calls, dispute handling, and bank reconciliation

Chaser offers 10% off on annual plans.

The structural detail to watch on Chaser's pricing is the Compact tier ceiling. At £199/month you get 4 users, 30 templates, and 4 workflows. Many growing teams hit one of those caps quickly and end up on the Core plan at £599/month, which is the real entry point for unconstrained use.

Yonovo publishes its pricing in USD on [the pricing page](/pricing), includes a free tier for low-volume teams, and offers flat paid plans that scale with usage rather than revenue bracket. Multi-channel outreach (email, SMS, voice, WhatsApp) is included.

**The takeaway:** if you want a free tier to validate the workflow before paying, Yonovo is the lower-friction path. If you are a UK business comfortable with GBP pricing and your volume justifies the Core tier or higher, Chaser is a credible option.

## Setup and time to value

Yonovo connects to your accounting system in a matter of minutes. Templates and workflows are pre-built and customizable from day one. [Troyes](/case-studies/troyes) went from fully manual to fully automated in a single day. [TDG Inc](/case-studies/tdg-inc) reduced manual follow-ups by 80% and cut DSO by 15 days within three months of switching.

Chaser does not publish a typical implementation timeline. Its Compact and Core tiers do not include onboarding services, while Complete adds "assisted onboarding" with a dedicated account manager. Reviews on G2 and Capterra mention a learning curve on the more advanced features, which is consistent with a platform that has accumulated depth over time.

If you have implementation bandwidth and want to invest in configuring forecasting and credit monitoring, Chaser rewards that investment. If your finance team is already behind on closing the books, Yonovo's same-day setup is the meaningful difference.

## Channels and outreach

Both platforms cover the major outreach channels.

Chaser supports email, SMS, auto-call, and postal letters, with an AI email generator for personalized messages and recommended chasing times based on payer behavior.

Yonovo supports email, SMS, voice, and WhatsApp as first-class channels in the workflow engine. You can design escalation paths that move from email at day 1 to SMS at day 14 to voice at day 30, with all channels included in the plan.

This is the area where the two platforms are most similar. The practical difference comes down to which channels matter most for your customer base and which currency makes more sense for your billing.

For a fuller breakdown of why multi-channel matters, see our guide on [multi-channel payment chasing](/blog/multi-channel-payment-chasing).

## Integrations

Both platforms cover the major accounting systems. Each one has areas of particular strength.

**Chaser** integrates with Xero, QuickBooks Online, Sage (50, 200, Intacct), SAP, Microsoft Dynamics 365, AccountsIQ, Odoo, NetSuite, plus CRMs and helpdesks like HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, Zoho, and Zendesk. The Xero integration is the most often cited strength in reviews, which lines up with Chaser's UK and accountant-channel positioning.

**Yonovo** integrates with [QuickBooks Online](/solutions/quickbooks), [Xero](/solutions/xero), [Odoo](/solutions/odoo), [NetSuite](/solutions/netsuite), [Sage Intacct](/solutions/sage), FreshBooks, Salesforce, HubSpot, and SAP. Sync is real time and two-way, with a check before every reminder to avoid chasing invoices that were paid overnight. Coverage is particularly strong for QuickBooks-centric US businesses and for finance teams that run Salesforce or HubSpot as their invoicing source of record.

If your stack is Xero-first, both work and Chaser has a slight edge in ecosystem familiarity. If your stack is QuickBooks plus a US CRM, Yonovo is the more direct path.

## Forecasting and reporting

This is the area where Chaser is genuinely differentiated, and it is fair to acknowledge that directly.

Chaser includes revenue forecast, receivables forecast, and cash flow forecast tools, with receivables forecast available on the Complete tier. For finance teams that want a unified view of incoming cash, Chaser folds that into the same platform as collections. That matters if your CFO or board wants forecasting and AR in one tool.

Yonovo focuses on operational AR reporting: aging dashboards, DSO tracking, collection effectiveness metrics, and a daily briefing that summarizes activity, follow-ups sent, and cash recovered. The reporting is built for operational decisions, specifically what your AR team needs to do today, rather than peer benchmarking or rolling cash flow forecasts.

**If forecasting is the primary reason you are evaluating, Chaser leads here.** If your priority is automation that runs in the background and only surfaces exceptions, Yonovo is more focused on that.

## Outsourced collections (Chaser Care)

Chaser also offers a service tier called Care, from £324/month, that provides a dedicated AR specialist to handle payment follow-ups, phone calls, inbox management, dispute resolution, and bank reconciliation on your behalf. This is collections-as-a-service layered on top of the software.

Yonovo does not currently offer an outsourced AR specialist service. Yonovo is built on the assumption that the right answer for a growing business is automation that does the work, not a person who does it manually with software help.

If you want a hybrid of software plus a human chasing payments for you, Chaser's Care is the more direct option. If you want full automation and you do not want a service line item on top of the software, Yonovo is purpose-built for that.

## Who should choose Chaser

- UK or European SMB businesses comfortable with GBP pricing
- Teams running Xero as the accounting source of truth
- Finance teams that value built-in cash flow and receivables forecasting
- Teams that want the option to outsource collections to a dedicated AR specialist
- Accountants and bookkeepers serving multiple clients through Chaser's partner program

## Who should choose Yonovo

- US-based SMB and mid-market businesses across industries
- Finance teams that need collections running by end of day, not end of quarter
- Teams that want a free tier and published USD pricing
- Businesses on [QuickBooks](/solutions/quickbooks), [Xero](/solutions/xero), [Odoo](/solutions/odoo), [Sage](/solutions/sage), [NetSuite](/solutions/netsuite), FreshBooks, Salesforce, HubSpot, or SAP
- Organizations chasing many invoices where same-day onboarding makes the difference between automating this quarter or next quarter
- Businesses with seasonal, project-based, or industry-varied billing, not just subscription revenue

## How to decide

**Start with your accounting system.** If you are deep in Xero and value the ecosystem fit, Chaser has the slight edge. If you are on QuickBooks, NetSuite, Sage, or running invoicing out of Salesforce or HubSpot, Yonovo is the more direct path.

**Check your geography and billing currency.** If you are a UK or European business, Chaser's GBP pricing and UK operations are a natural fit. If you are a US business, Yonovo prices in USD and operates in the US market.

**Decide if you need cash flow forecasting in the same tool.** Chaser bundles forecasting with collections. Yonovo separates them, focusing on operational AR and leaving forecasting to dedicated finance tools. There is no wrong answer, but the right choice depends on whether your CFO wants one platform or two.

**Decide if you want a human in the loop.** Chaser's Care add-on is real and serves a real need. If you would rather have a dedicated AR specialist chasing on your behalf than configure more automation, that pushes you toward Chaser.

**Be honest about budget and time.** If you want to validate a tool with no commitment, Yonovo's free tier gets you to a working workflow today. If you are comfortable starting at £199/month and have implementation bandwidth, Chaser is workable.

**Run both in parallel.** Both platforms sit on top of your accounting system, so you can connect each to a sandbox or test environment, configure one workflow, and see which one feels like it runs itself.
